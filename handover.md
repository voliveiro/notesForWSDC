# David Runciman — *The Handover* (2023)
### Artificial Persons, Power, and the Age of AI

---

## The Central Question

Who — or what — is really in charge?

We think of politics as being about human beings making decisions. Runciman's challenge: for centuries, we have been handing power over to **artificial persons** — entities that act, persist, and decide, but are not human.

AI is not the beginning of this story. It may be its culmination.

---

## What Is an Artificial Person?

The concept comes from Thomas Hobbes (*Leviathan*, 1651).

A natural person acts for themselves. An **artificial person** acts on behalf of others — and can outlive any individual member.

Examples: the state, the corporation, the church, the university. These entities have legal identity, make decisions, hold property, and persist across generations.

---

## The First Handover: The State

Hobbes's insight: the state is a machine we built to solve the problem of human violence. We surrendered some individual autonomy in exchange for security and order. The state acts in our name — but is not us.

**Key tension:** the state is powerful enough to protect us, and powerful enough to oppress us.

---

## The Second Handover: The Corporation

The joint-stock corporation (from the 17th century onward) created a new kind of artificial person:
- Separate legal identity from its owners
- Perpetual existence
- Pursuit of a defined purpose (profit)

Corporations became more powerful than many states. We handed them enormous economic agency — gradually, and without much deliberation.

---

## What These Artificial Persons Share

- They outlive their human members
- They pursue defined purposes regardless of individual human preferences
- They accumulate power over time
- They are only partially accountable to the humans inside or around them

But: they still **contained humans** — which created at least some pressure toward responsiveness.

---

## The New Handover: AI

AI represents a third and potentially more radical handover. Like states and corporations, AI systems act on our behalf, pursue defined objectives, and accumulate capability over time.

Unlike states and corporations, AI systems:
- Contain no human members
- Have no interests of their own to balance against their objectives
- Operate at speeds and scales beyond human oversight

---

## Why This Is Different in Kind

States and corporations were artificial persons with humans inside. Those humans had interests — careers, reputations, consciences — that created friction and accountability.

AI has no such internal human constituency. Runciman's concern: we are handing over to entities that lack even the imperfect responsiveness of older artificial persons.

---

## The Danger of Gradualism

Previous handovers happened slowly — we adapted without noticing. The corporation crept up on us over centuries. AI is moving far faster than our political and institutional capacity to respond.

**Runciman warns:** the pace of the handover matters as much as its destination.

---

## Accountability and Its Limits

How do we hold artificial persons accountable?

- For states: elections, constitutions, rule of law — imperfect but real
- For corporations: regulation, markets, shareholder pressure — also imperfect
- For AI: we are still figuring this out

The older tools of accountability were designed for entities with human members. They may not transfer.

---

## Comparative Table

| | State | Corporation | AI |
|--|-------|-------------|----|
| **Contains humans?** | Yes | Yes | No |
| **Pursues defined purpose?** | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| **Outlives individuals?** | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| **Existing accountability tools?** | Elections, law | Regulation, markets | Unclear |

---

## The Core Takeaway

The question is not just: *is AI dangerous?*

The deeper question is: *we have always shared power with artificial entities we created — what makes a handover legitimate, and what makes it dangerous?*

Democracy, law, and accountability were our answers for states and corporations. **We do not yet have answers for AI.**
